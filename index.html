Learn the basics

Bite-sized explanations of the building blocks Quantara uses in your portfolio.

ETFs

Exchange Traded Funds

A basket of many shares bundled together. Instead of buying one company, you buy a small piece of hundreds — spreading your risk automatically across an entire market or sector.

Shares

Individual company ownership

Buying a share means owning a small piece of a company. Shares can offer strong growth but move up and down more than other assets, so they suit longer time horizons.

Bonds

Loans that pay you interest

A bond is essentially a loan to a government or company, who pays you interest in return. Bonds are generally lower risk and help stabilise a portfolio during downturns.

Risk tolerance

How much fluctuation you can handle

Risk tolerance reflects how comfortable you are with your portfolio's value moving up and down in the short term, in exchange for potentially higher long-term returns.

Time horizon

When you'll need the money

The longer your money can stay invested, the more time it has to recover from short-term drops — which is why time horizon shapes how much risk makes sense for you.

Diversification

Not putting all your eggs in one basket

Spreading investments across asset classes reduces the impact of any single investment performing poorly, smoothing out your overall returns over time.

About Quantara

Quantara exists because investment advice in Australia is broken for the people who need it most.

Many Australians face significant barriers when attempting to access individually tailored investment advice. Traditional financial advisors charge a median of $4,668 per year — out of reach for low to middle income earners and those new to investing. Generic online resources fill some of the gap, but they're a one-size-fits-all product, not personal guidance.

Quantara uses AI to generate a personalised portfolio recommendation based on your actual profile — age, goals, risk tolerance and time horizon — and explains every recommendation in plain English. It's built for young Australians, university students, and first-time investors who want to start, but don't know where.

What Quantara isn't: a licensed financial advisor, a trading platform, or a tax advisory service. It's a starting point — accessible, transparent, and free.
